Causal Discovery
on World Development
Indices

Rich datasets like the one on world development indices can lead to a paradox of choice. When we had very little data on anything, we were more certain about what kind of questions we should be asking because we had fewer questions that were answerable. We made a wish on a monkey's paw and asked for more data, and we were granted a deluge of data so large that we're behind on our ability to make sense of it, to meaningfully separate signal from noise. When we're given a dataset, how do we decide which patterns matter and which are probably a coincidence?

Correlation
